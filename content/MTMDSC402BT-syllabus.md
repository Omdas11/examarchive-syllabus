Course Objective 
The objective of this course is to provide learners with an advanced understanding of 
mathematical analysis. It covers topics like convergence of functions, power series, 
functions of bounded variations, and elementary measure theory. 
Unit-I 
Pointwise and uniform convergence of sequence and series of functions: definitions, 
examples and related problems. Cauchy's criterion for uniform convergence and 
Weierstrass M-Test. 
Unit-II 
Limit superior and limit inferior, power series, radius of convergence, Cauchy Hadamard 
theorem. Applications and related problems. 
Unit-III 
Functions of bounded variations: definition, examples, and properties; total variation. 
Unit-IV 
o-algebra, Borel sets and Borel o-algebra. Lebesgue outer measure and measurable sets: 
definition, examples and properties. 
Unit-V 
Lebesgue measure, its properties, and the Borel-Cantelli lemma. Cantor set: 
Construction and topological properties.

Course Outcomes: 
On completion of the course, learners will be able to analyse and demonstrate 
convergence properties of functions, determine the convergence of power series, and 
apply the idea of functions of bounded variations in various contexts. They will also 
master essential concepts in measure theory, such as o-algebras and Lebesgue 
measure, and apply them to analyse measurable sets.
